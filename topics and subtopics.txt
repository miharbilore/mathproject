Unit 0: Foundations of Algebra
Variables and Expressions / Order of Operations
Students will be able to evaluate numerical expressions using the correct order of operations (PEMDAS).
Students will be able to translate verbal phrases into algebraic expressions.
Students will be able to simplify algebraic expressions by combining like terms.
Students will be able to substitute given values into expressions and evaluate results.
Natural Numbers & Integers
Students will be able to classify numbers (natural, whole, integer).
Students will be able to identify and generate even and odd numbers.
Students will be able to recognize and extend numerical patterns.
Students will be able to perform operations with integers, including negatives.
Rational Numbers & Decimals
Students will be able to convert between fractions, decimals, and percents.
Students will be able to compare and order rational numbers.
Students will be able to perform four operations with fractions and decimals.
Students will be able to represent rational numbers on a number line.
Place Value
Students will be able to identify the value of digits in multi-digit numbers.
Students will be able to round numbers to a specified place.
Students will be able to write numbers in expanded and standard form.
Divisibility & Division Algorithm
Students will be able to apply divisibility rules (2, 3, 5, 9, 10).
Students will be able to perform long division.
Students will be able to express division results with remainders.
GCF & LCM
Students will be able to find GCF using factors or prime factorization.
Students will be able to find LCM using multiples or prime factorization.
Students will be able to solve real-world problems involving GCF and LCM.
Equations
Students will be able to solve one-step equations.
Students will be able to solve multi-step linear equations.
Students will be able to check solutions for accuracy.
Isolating Variables
Students will be able to rearrange formulas to isolate a given variable.
Students will be able to solve literal equations.
Inequalities
Students will be able to solve one- and multi-step inequalities.
Students will be able to graph solutions on a number line.
Students will be able to write solutions in interval notation.




📘 Unit 1: Ratio, Rate, Percentage
Percent
Students will be able to calculate percentages and percent increase/decrease.
Students will be able to solve real-world percent problems.
Ratios & Proportions
Students will be able to represent ratios in multiple forms.
Students will be able to solve proportions using cross multiplication.
Students will be able to model real-world situations with proportions.
Mixture Problems
Students will be able to solve mixture problems using equations.
Students will be able to interpret concentration and ratios.
Rates & Variation
Students will be able to calculate unit rates.
Students will be able to convert units.
Students will be able to model direct and inverse variation.
Students will be able to solve rate problems (speed, work, etc.).




📘 Unit 2: Exponents & Radicals
Exponents
Students will be able to apply exponent rules (product, quotient, power).
Students will be able to simplify expressions with exponents.
Exponential Equations
Students will be able to solve basic exponential equations.
Radicals
Students will be able to simplify square and cube roots.
Students will be able to convert between radical and rational exponent form.
Operations with Radicals
Students will be able to add/subtract like radicals.
Students will be able to multiply/divide radicals.
Rationalizing Denominator
Students will be able to rationalize denominators involving radicals.



Unit 3: Functions
Definition of Relation and Function, Domain, Range, and Notations
Students will be able to distinguish between relations and functions using ordered pairs, tables, graphs, and mappings.
Students will be able to determine whether a relation is a function using the vertical line test.
Students will be able to identify domain and range from graphs, tables, and equations.
Students will be able to evaluate functions using proper function notation (e.g., f(x)).
Operations with Functions
Students will be able to perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of functions.
Students will be able to determine the domain of combined functions.
Students will be able to simplify resulting expressions after operations.
Composite Functions
Students will be able to evaluate composite functions (f(g(x))).
Students will be able to determine domain restrictions for composite functions.
Students will be able to interpret compositions in real-world contexts.
Function Transformations
Students will be able to describe and apply vertical and horizontal shifts.
Students will be able to apply reflections over x-axis and y-axis.
Students will be able to apply stretches and compressions.
Students will be able to compare transformed graphs to parent functions.
Inverse Relations and Functions
Students will be able to determine if a function has an inverse.
Students will be able to find inverse functions algebraically.
Students will be able to verify inverses using composition.
Students will be able to interpret inverses in real-world contexts.



📘 Unit 4: Absolute Value
Definition of Absolute Value
Students will be able to define absolute value as distance from zero on a number line.
Students will be able to evaluate absolute value expressions.
Students will be able to interpret absolute value in contextual problems.
Solving Absolute Value Equations
Students will be able to solve equations of the form |x| = a.
Students will be able to solve multi-step absolute value equations.
Students will be able to check solutions for extraneous results.
Absolute Value Function and its Graph
Students will be able to graph absolute value functions.
Students will be able to identify vertex, axis of symmetry, and direction of opening.
Students will be able to apply transformations to absolute value graphs.
Absolute Value Inequalities and Their Applications
Students will be able to solve inequalities involving absolute value.
Students will be able to express solutions in interval notation.
Students will be able to graph solution sets on a number line.
Students will be able to model real-world problems using absolute value inequalities.



📘 Unit 5: Linear Equations
Rate of Change and Slope
Students will be able to calculate slope from graphs, tables, and equations.
Students will be able to interpret slope as rate of change.
Students will be able to identify positive, negative, zero, and undefined slopes.
Point-Slope Form, Slope-Intercept Form, Standard Form and Converting Forms
Students will be able to write linear equations in different forms.
Students will be able to convert between forms.
Students will be able to graph linear equations from each form.
Students will be able to interpret slope and intercepts in context.
Parallel Lines
Students will be able to identify parallel lines using slope.
Students will be able to write equations of parallel lines.
Students will be able to analyze relationships between parallel lines.
Perpendicular Lines
Students will be able to identify perpendicular lines using negative reciprocal slopes.
Students will be able to write equations of perpendicular lines.
Students will be able to apply perpendicular relationships in problem solving.
Scatter Plot and The Best Fit Line
Students will be able to create scatter plots.
Students will be able to identify correlation (positive, negative, none).
Students will be able to draw and interpret a line of best fit.
Students will be able to make predictions based on data trends.



📘 Unit 6: Systems of Linear Equations and Inequalities
Solving Systems Using Elimination and Substitution
Students will be able to solve systems using substitution.
Students will be able to solve systems using elimination.
Students will be able to determine if a system has one solution, no solution, or infinitely many solutions.
Applications of Systems (Word Problems)
Students will be able to translate real-world problems into systems of equations.
Students will be able to interpret solutions in context.
Students will be able to evaluate reasonableness of solutions.
Solving Systems by Graphing
Students will be able to graph linear systems.
Students will be able to identify intersection points as solutions.
Students will be able to interpret graphical solutions.
Linear Inequalities with Two Variables
Students will be able to graph linear inequalities in two variables.
Students will be able to identify solution regions.
Students will be able to distinguish between solid and dashed boundary lines.
Systems of Linear Inequalities by Graphing
Students will be able to graph multiple inequalities on the same coordinate plane.
Students will be able to identify overlapping solution regions.
Students will be able to interpret feasible regions.
Applications of Systems of Inequalities
Students will be able to model real-world constraints using inequalities.
Students will be able to interpret feasible solutions in context.
Students will be able to optimize within constraint regions (intro level).



📘 Unit 7: Factoring
Finding Common Factors
Students will be able to factor out the greatest common factor.
Students will be able to simplify expressions using factoring.
Factoring by Grouping
Students will be able to factor four-term expressions by grouping.
Students will be able to verify factoring by multiplication.
Difference of Squares
Students will be able to recognize and factor expressions in the form a² − b².
Students will be able to apply this pattern in problem solving.
Expanding Special Patterns & Perfect Square Trinomial
Students will be able to expand binomials using special patterns.
Students will be able to recognize perfect square trinomials.
Students will be able to factor perfect square trinomials.
Factoring Trinomials
Students will be able to factor trinomials of the form ax² + bx + c.
Students will be able to apply different factoring methods.
Sum and Difference of Cubes
Students will be able to recognize cube patterns.
Students will be able to factor expressions of the form a³ ± b³.



📘 Unit 8: Quadratics
Definition of Quadratics and Standard Form
Students will be able to identify quadratic functions in standard form.
Students will be able to describe key features of quadratic functions.
Vertex Form and Intercept Form
Students will be able to write quadratic functions in vertex form.
Students will be able to write quadratic functions in intercept form.
Students will be able to interpret parameters in each form.
Graphing Quadratics and Properties of Parabolas
Students will be able to graph parabolas.
Students will be able to identify vertex, axis of symmetry, intercepts, and direction.
Students will be able to analyze maximum and minimum values.
Converting Forms (Completing the Square)
Students will be able to convert standard form to vertex form.
Students will be able to complete the square.
Students will be able to interpret transformed equations.
Solving by Factoring and Other Methods
Students will be able to solve quadratic equations by factoring.
Students will be able to verify solutions.
Solving by Quadratic Formula (Discriminant)
Students will be able to apply the quadratic formula.
Students will be able to interpret the discriminant.
Students will be able to determine number and type of solutions.
Systems of Linear and Quadratic Equations
Students will be able to solve systems involving linear and quadratic equations.
Students will be able to interpret intersection points.
Systems of Quadratic Equations
Students will be able to solve systems involving two quadratic equations.
Students will be able to interpret graphical solutions.
